Pteranodon
Longiceps

Late Cretaceous · 86–84 Million Years Ago

One of the largest flying reptiles ever discovered, the Pteranodon soared above shallow inland seas with a wingspan rivaling a modern small aircraft. This specimen, excavated from the Niobrara Chalk of Kansas, remains among the most complete pterosaur fossils in existence — a window into the age of winged giants.

[ 03 ] — Geological Record

Through
Deep Time

Triassic Period
First Dinosaurs Emerge
252 — 201 million years ago

Small bipedal dinosaurs first appear in Gondwana. Eoraptor and Herrerasaurus pioneer a lineage that will dominate the planet for 165 million years.

Jurassic Period
Giants Dominate the Earth
201 — 145 million years ago

Sauropods like Brachiosaurus and Diplodocus reach staggering sizes. The Allosaurus terrorizes the landscape. First birds appear — feathered theropods taking flight.

Early Cretaceous
Diversification Era
145 — 100 million years ago

Flowering plants emerge transforming ecosystems. Spinosaurus prowls river deltas. The stage is set for the most iconic predators in Earth's history.

Late Cretaceous · Apex
Age of the T-Rex
100 — 66 million years ago

Tyrannosaurus Rex reigns supreme. Triceratops, Stegosaurus and Pteranodon share a world of tremendous scale. Herds of hadrosaurs stretch across continents.

K-Pg Extinction Event
The Great Silence
66 million years ago

A 10 km asteroid strikes Chicxulub, triggering a nuclear winter. 75% of all species perish. The age of the dinosaurs ends in fire, dust and darkness.
